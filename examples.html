Claude
The focus group results are in. This is interesting — it's not a simple winner:

Concept A (Performance): 6.2/10 overall
• Strong with competitive runners (8.1/10) — "This speaks my language"
• Weak with casual runners (4.3/10) — "Feels intimidating, like it's not for me"
• High purchase intent among serious runners, but narrow audience

Concept B (Lifestyle): 7.4/10 overall
• Strong across all segments — floor of 6.5, ceiling of 8.2
• Casual runners loved it (8.2/10) — "Makes me want to just go run"
• Competitive runners still responded well (6.8/10) — "Nice vibe but I want to see the tech"

Key Insight: 6 of 8 participants suggested a hybrid — lead with lifestyle imagery but include one performance proof point (e.g., "worn by 3 Boston Marathon qualifiers"). This tested as the strongest positioning in follow-up reactions.

Recommendation: Go with Concept B as the primary creative, but add a performance-credibility element. This gives you the broadest appeal without alienating serious runners.
